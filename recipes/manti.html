Manti

Description

Manti are traditional Uzbek dumplings filled with spiced ground meat, typically lamb or beef, and finely chopped onions.

These savory parcels are often seasoned with cumin, coriander, and black pepper, providing a burst of flavor in each bite.

Manti are usually steamed until tender and served hot, sometimes garnished with fresh herbs or accompanied by a dollop of yogurt.

They are a beloved dish in Uzbek cuisine, enjoyed for their rich taste and satisfying texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 lb (450 g) ground lamb or beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1/4 cup water (for filling)

Steps

  1. Prepare Dough: In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Gradually add warm water, mixing until a dough forms. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make Filling: In a separate bowl, combine the ground meat, chopped onion, minced garlic, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground black pepper, and salt. Mix well. Add 1/4 cup water to make the filling juicy.
  3. Divide Dough: Divide the rested dough into small, equal-sized balls (about the size of a golf ball).
  4. Roll Out Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll each dough ball into a thin circle (about 4-5 inches in diameter).
  5. Add Filling: Place a spoonful of the meat filling in the center of each dough circle.
  6. Seal Manti: Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape, pressing the edges together to seal. You can also pinch and pleat the edges for a decorative touch.
  7. Steam Manti: Place the manti in a steamer basket, ensuring they do not touch each other. Steam over boiling water for about 30-40 minutes, until the dough is cooked and the filling is tender.
  8. Serve: Serve hot, optionally garnished with chopped fresh herbs or a dollop of yogurt.
